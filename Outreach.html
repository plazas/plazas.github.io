Outreach

My commitment to science education and public outreach is deeply rooted in my belief that knowledge should be accessible to all. Through various initiatives, I aim to inspire curiosity and foster a greater understanding of the universe among diverse audiences. Below are some of the key outreach activities and projects I am involved in.

Podcast: Visión Cósmica

I am the creator and co-host of the Spanish-language astronomy podcast Visión Cósmica. This podcast aims to bring the wonders of the cosmos to Spanish-speaking audiences around the world, offering discussions on the latest discoveries, interviews with scientists, and explanations of fundamental concepts in astronomy.

Selected Outreach Activities

  • Astronomy on Tap (St. Louis & Trenton, NJ): I founded the Astronomy on Tap satellite branches in St. Louis and Trenton, where I organize events that bring astronomy talks to informal settings, like bars and cafes, making science accessible and engaging for the public.
  • Tower Grove Park Astronomy Festival: As a proposer and co-organizer, I helped create the first-ever astronomy festival in Tower Grove Park, St. Louis. The event featured telescope viewings, talks, and activities for all ages, introducing attendees to the wonders of the night sky.
  • Outreach in Spanish at Harvard College Observatory: I co-organized the first-ever outreach event in Spanish at the Harvard College Observatory, creating an inclusive space for Spanish-speaking communities to engage with astronomy.
  • NASA JPL Solar System Ambassador: As a volunteer Solar System Ambassador, I participate in various educational events, sharing NASA’s latest missions and discoveries with the public, particularly in Spanish-speaking communities.
  • CosmoQuest: I was involved with the CosmoQuest project as a Research Scientist at the Astronomical Society of the Pacific. My role included mentoring community scientists in planetary science projects like crater counting and analysis.

Through these initiatives and more, I aim to continue spreading the joy of discovery and the importance of science to communities both local and global.
